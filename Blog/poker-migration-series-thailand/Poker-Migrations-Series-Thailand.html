Cost of living:

Whether you're planning an extended holiday or pondering a digital nomad lifestyle, Phuket emerges as an excellent choice. Known for its awe-inspiring beaches, friendly locals, and cost-effective living expenses, the island offers a wide range of housing options. The cost of accommodation can significantly vary based on location, type of property, and season. Here's what you might expect to pay for housing on a monthly basis:

Hotels:

Extended stays at hotels in Phuket are certainly viable. A basic hotel room could cost between 15,000 to 25,000 baht per month ($450 - $750). For mid-range hotels, expect to pay between 25,000 to 50,000 baht per month ($750 - $1,500). Luxury hotels and resorts, however, may exceed 100,000 baht ($3,000) or more per month.

Serviced Apartments:

Serviced apartments are a popular choice for long stays, offering extras like cleaning services, utilities, and access to amenities such as a gym or pool. A standard serviced apartment might cost around 10,000 to 20,000 baht per month ($300 - $600), while high-end options can range from 30,000 to 60,000 baht ($900 - $1,800) or more per month.

Airbnb:

Airbnb rentals can vary widely in cost. A private room may start from around 7,000 baht ($210) per month. If you prefer a place all to yourself, prices generally start at around 15,000 baht ($450) for a simple apartment. For a luxurious villa complete with a private pool and other amenities, you might need to budget upwards of 60,000 baht ($1,800) per month.

Rental Apartments and Houses:

If you're planning a long-term stay in Phuket, renting an apartment or house may be the most cost-effective option. A basic one-bedroom apartment in a less tourist-centric area could cost around 8,000 to 15,000 baht per month ($240 - $450). In contrast, a similar apartment in a more bustling area like Patong could range between 15,000 to 30,000 baht ($450 - $900). Standalone houses or villas could start from around 20,000 baht ($600) and escalate to 100,000 baht ($3,000) or more for luxury options.

Please note, these prices are estimations and can fluctuate based on location, season, and the amenities provided. Always remember to check multiple sources, read reviews, and negotiate when possible to secure the best deal.

Phuket's Guide to Grocery Prices for Visitors

Known not only for its magnificent beaches, Phuket is also renowned for its affordable prices. However, it's essential to understand that grocery costs can greatly vary depending on whether you're shopping in a tourist district or a local supermarket. Here's a guide to grocery prices in Phuket to help you plan your budget:

Restaurants and Street Food Stalls:

Phuket offers an abundant selection of dining options, from upscale restaurants serving international cuisine to street food stalls dishing out local favorites. A meal at a mid-range restaurant costs around 200-500 baht ($6 to $15), while a meal at a street food stall typically ranges between 50 and 100 baht ($1.5 to $3).

Supermarkets:

You can find a vast range of groceries at Phuket's supermarkets and grocery stores. Here are some sample prices (in baht):

  • Bread (500g) - around 40-60
  • Milk (1l) - around 40-50
  • Eggs (12 units) - around 40-50
  • Cheese (500g) - around 150-300
  • Apples (1kg) - around 60-100
  • Bottle of water (1.5l) - around 15-20
  • Local beer (0.5l) - around 40-60

Local markets often offer lower prices than supermarkets, especially for fresh fruits, vegetables, and seafood. Remember, though, haggling is common practice here and is often expected.

Alcohol:

Much like many places worldwide, alcohol is generally more expensive in bars and restaurants. A bottle of local beer in a bar costs around 60-100 baht ($2 to $3), while a bottle of imported beer costs between 100 and 180 baht ($3 to $5.5). Cocktails are usually more expensive, with prices starting from around 150 baht ($4.5).

In conclusion, Phuket caters to a range of food preferences and budgets. Local cuisine is typically inexpensive and flavorful, and grocery stores are well-stocked for those who prefer to cook at home. Please bear in mind, these prices are approximate and may vary depending on the season and location.

Tourist attractions:

Phuket, Thailand's largest island and a well-known tourist destination, is famous for its beautiful beaches, clear waters, vibrant marine life, and an array of activities. Here are some key attractions to consider

Phuket is celebrated for its stunning beaches with white sand and turquoise waters. Some of the most well-known are Patong, Kata, Karon, Kamala, Bang Tao, and Surin. Patong is the busiest, buzzing with bars, restaurants, and nightclubs, while Kata and Karon offer more tranquillity.

Phuket Old Town is a picturesque area filled with Sino-Portuguese style buildings. You'll find numerous shops, restaurants, bars, and historical buildings such as Chinese temples, museums, and the town hall.

The Big Buddha is a monumental Buddha statue situated atop Nakkerd Hill, offering breathtaking views of Phuket.

The scenic Phi Phi Islands make for a fantastic day trip from Phuket. Activities here include scuba diving, swimming, sunbathing, and visiting the filming location of Leonardo DiCaprio's movie, "The Beach

Scuba Diving:

Phuket is a gateway to some of the world's top diving sites, including the Similan Islands and Richelieu Rock. You can explore vibrant coral reefs, encounter a variety of marine life, and even take diving lessons if you're a beginner.

Phang Nga Bay:

Phang Nga Bay is famous for its limestone cliffs jutting out of emerald-green waters. A popular way to explore this area is by taking a kayak tour or renting a longtail boat.

Thai Boxing:

Muay Thai, or Thai Boxing, is the national sport of Thailand. You can watch a match in various stadiums around the island or even take a class to learn the basics of this martial art.

Night Markets:

Visiting the night markets is a must when in Phuket. From clothing and souvenirs to delicious street food, you can find almost anything. Some of the most popular ones include Phuket Weekend Market and Malin Plaza Patong.
